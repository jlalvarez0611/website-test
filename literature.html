Title: Noli Me Tangere (1887)

Type: Novel

Langage: Spanish

Description: : A farewell poem written by Rizal before his execution, reflecting his love for the Philippines and hope for its freedom.

Significance: Often called the "Touch Me Not," the novel exposed societal injustices and inspired the Philippine revolution.

Notable Characters: Crisostomo Ibarra, María Clara, Padre Damaso, Elias.

Title: El Filibusterismo (1891)

Type: Novel

Langage: Spanish

Description: A darker sequel to Noli Me Tangere, focusing on the themes of revenge and revolution.

Significance: : It highlighted the futility of reform under the colonial system and advocated for radical change.

Notable Characters: Simoun (Crisostomo Ibarra), Basilio, Padre Florentino, Isagani.

Title: Mi Último Adiós (1896)

Type: Poetry

Langage: Spanish

Description: A farewell poem written by Rizal before his execution, reflecting his love for the Philippines and hope for its freedom.

Significance: Considered one of the greatest patriotic poems in Philippine history.

Title: A La Juventud Filipina (1879)

Type: Poem

Description: A poem dedicated to Filipino youth, urging them to aspire to greatness.

Significance: It won Rizal his first literary prize at Ateneo.

Title: El Canto del Viajero (The Song of the Traveler)

Type: Poem

Langage: Spanish

Description: A reflective poem about his travels and longing for home.

Significance: It expresses Rizal's patriotism and his bittersweet experiences abroad.

Title: Hymn to Labor

Title: The Indolence of the Filipinos

Type: Essay

Langage: Spanish

Description: A critical essay analyzing the reasons behind the supposed laziness of Filipinos, which Rizal attributed to colonial oppression.

Significance: Challenges colonial stereotypes and highlights the structural issues caused by Spanish rule.

Title: Letter to the Women of Malolos

Type: Essay

Langage: Tagalog

Description: A letter encouraging Filipino women to fight for their rights and educate themselves.

Significance: A call for gender equality and empowerment during the colonial period.
