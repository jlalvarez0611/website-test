  • Sculpture
    Mother’s Revenge Prometheus Bound The Triumph of Science over Death Sacred Heart of Jesus St. Paul the Hermit

Title: Mother’s Revenge

Description: A sculpture of a mother dog defending her puppy from a crocodile attack.

Significance: This represents maternal love and courage. Rizal created this while exiled in Dapitan as a tribute to the sacrifices of mothers.

Title: Prometheus Bound

Description: Rizal sculpted a figure of Prometheus, the mythological Greek hero bound as punishment for giving fire to humanity. Created in 1890, this sculpture symbolizes Rizal's desire to remove ignorance from his countrymen after 300 years of spanish rules.

Significance: This piece symbolizes Rizal's struggles and his sacrifice for enlightening his people, akin to the legend of Prometheus.

Title: The Triumph of Science over Death

Description: This clay sculpture features a young woman standing triumphantly, symbolizing science, while a skull, representing death, lies beneath her feet.

Significance: Created in 1890 in Dapitan, it reflects Rizal's belief in the power of science and knowledge to conquer ignorance and mortality.

Title: Sacred Heart of Jesus

Description: A wooden sculpture of the Sacred Heart of Jesus that Rizal carved as a student at Ateneo Municipal de Manila. Rizal carved this famous work while he was a student in Ateneo Municipal in Intramuros.

Significance: This piece was displayed prominently at Ateneo and symbolized Rizal's spiritual side, despite his later criticism of the clergy.

Title: San Pablo Ermitano (St. Paul the Hermit)(The Song of the Traveler)

>

Description: A statue of St. Paul the Hermit holding a crucifix and seated in meditation.

Significance: Rizal sculpted this during his teenage years, showcasing his early talent for religious and figurative art.
