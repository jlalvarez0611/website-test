Introduction to José Rizal

José Protasio Rizal Mercado y
Alonso Realonda

Born: June 19, 1861, Calamba, Philippines
Died: December 30, 1896, Manila (aged 35)

José Protasio Rizal Mercado y Alonso Realonda was a Filipino nationalist, writer, and polymath active at the end of the Spanish colonial period of the Philippines. He is considered a national hero (pambansang bayani) of the Philippines. An ophthalmologist by profession, Rizal became a writer and a key member of the Filipino Propaganda Movement, which advocated political reforms for the colony under Spain.

Rizal's Early Life

CHILDHOOD

Rizal spent his early childhood in the town of Calamba. He had many fond memories of exploring the forests and rivers near his home. Rizal also had a strong religious upbringing, regularly attending church and prayers. He began writing poetry at a young age and showed an early talent for art and drama.

FAMILY BACKGROUND AND INFLUENCES

Rizal’s family played a crucial role in shaping his values, providing him with both the emotional and intellectual support needed to pursue his goals. His family, especially his parents and siblings, fostered an environment of learning and critical thinking that helped him become one of the most important figures in Philippine history. Their influence, combined with his exposure to progressive European ideas, paved the way for his vision of a free and just Philippines.
